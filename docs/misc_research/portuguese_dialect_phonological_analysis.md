## 2. Major Phonological Features by Region

### 2.1 RETROFLEX CONSONANTS — The Northern Signature

The most striking areal feature in the dataset is the presence of **retroflex consonants** [ʂ, ʐ, ɭ, ɽ], characteristic of northern and some central varieties. These derive historically from Latin clusters -LL-, -L- (via dark /ɫ/) and palatalized /s/ in certain environments.

#### 2.1.1 Retroflex Lateral [ɭ]

The retroflex lateral [ɭ] appears where standard Portuguese has /l/ (especially in onset or post-consonantal position). This is the hallmark of the **Beira Litoral** and **Alto Minho** dialect areas.

**Vagos, Aveiro** (heaviest attestation):
- *lá* → [ɭa]
- *global* → [ɣluˈβaɭɐ]
- *estimulando* → [ʃtimˈɭɐ̃d]
- *pimpinela* → [pĩpiˈɲɛɭɐ]

**Viana do Castelo:**
- *leituras* → [ɭejˈtuɾɐz]
- *lia* → [ˈɭiɐ]
- *claro* → [ˈkɭaɾu]
- *locutor* → [ɭukuˈtwoɾ]

**Madeira (Ribeira Brava):**
- *pimpinhela* → [pĩpiˈɲɛɭɐ]

This feature is ABSENT from Lisbon, Algarve, Azores, and Porto city proper.

#### 2.1.2 Retroflex Fricatives [ʂ] and [ʐ]

Voiceless [ʂ] and voiced [ʐ] appear as allophones of /s, z, ʃ, ʒ/ in specific environments (especially preconsonantal and word-final before a pause or voiceless onset). This is the **strongest diagnostic** of rural northern speech.

**Alvarenga, Porto** (most extreme — nearly every final /s/ is retroflex):
- *dois* → [ðojʐ]
- *estamos* → [ˈtɐmuʐɐ]
- *temos* → [ˈtemuʐ]
- *vamos* → [ˈβamuʐ]
- *nós* → [nɔʂ]
- *aqueles* → [ɐˈkelɨʐ]
- *seis* → [sejʂ]
- *dois* → [dojʂ]
- *clientes* → [kliˈẽtɨʐ]

**Vila Nova de Gaia:**
- *deserta* → [dˈzɛɾt̥ɐ̥] (but: *desenvolvidas* → [dzẽvɔɫˈviðɐʂ])
- *situações* → [sitwɐˈsõȷʂ̃]
- *respostas* → [xˈpɔʃtɐʂ]
- *serem* → [ˈʂeɾɐ̃ȷ̃]

**Penafiel:**
- *milhões* → [mɨˈʎojʐ]

**Viana do Castelo:**
- *anos* → [ˈɐnuʂ]
- *atrás* → [ɐˈtɾaʐ]
- *senhores* → [ʂiˈɲwɐɾʃ] (initial position!)

#### 2.1.3 Retroflex Flap [ɽ]

Occasional retroflex realization of the alveolar tap /ɾ/:

**Vizela, Braga:** *agora* → [ɐˈɣɔɽɐ]
**Penafiel:** *euro* → [ˈewɽɔ]
**Viana do Castelo:** *direções* → [diɽɛˈsõȷʃ̃]

### 2.2 VOWEL BREAKING / CENTERING DIPHTHONGIZATION — Alfena, Porto

**Alfena** shows a highly distinctive phenomenon: monophthongs break into **centering diphthongs** with a schwa-like offglide [ɐ]. This is transcribed with tie-bars in the data:

- *pé* → [ˈpɛ͡ɐ] (standard: [pɛ])
- *café* → [kɐˈfɛ͡ɐ] (standard: [kɐˈfɛ])
- *almoço* → [aɫˈmw͡ɐsu̥] (standard: [aɫˈmosu])
- *sono* → [ˈso͡ɐnu] (standard: [ˈsonu])
- *tempo* → [ˈtẽ͡ɐ̃pu̥] (standard: [ˈtẽpu])
- *sempre* → [sẽ͡ɐ̃p] (standard: [ˈsẽpɾɨ])
- *avô* → [ɐˈβwo͡ɐ] (standard: [ɐˈvo])

This **vowel breaking** (EP *Brechung*) is a recognized feature of certain Greater Porto varieties and parts of the Minho. It affects mid vowels [ɛ, ɔ, e, o] which develop a centering offglide, producing sequences like [ɛɐ], [oɐ], [ẽɐ̃]. This mirrors (in a completely independent way) the Leonese diphthongization of Latin short Ĕ and Ŏ — but here it extends to ALL mid vowels and is conditioned by stress rather than vowel quality.

Also noteworthy in Alfena: *planta* → [ˈpɭɒ̃t], [ˈpɭɒ̃tɐ] — with a backed rounded [ɒ̃] for /ɐ̃/, and retroflex lateral cluster [pɭ].

**This vowel-breaking pattern is unique to Alfena in this dataset.**

### 2.3 STOP ASPIRATION — Madeira

**Ribeira Brava (Madeira)** displays systematic **aspiration of voiceless stops** [pʰ, tʰ, kʰ], a feature virtually absent from mainland Portuguese but well documented in Madeiran:

- *hábito* → [ˈabitʰu] (final [t] aspirated)
- *morto* → [ˈmoɾtʰu]
- *frito* → [ˈfɾitʰu]
- *pronto* → [pɾɔ̃tʰ]
- *para* → [ˈpʰɐɾɐ] (initial [p] aspirated)
- *porco* → [ˈpoɾkʰu̥]
- *noite* → [nojtʰ]
- *tome* → [tʰom]
- *também* → [tʰɐˈmɐ̃ȷ̃]
- *depois* → [dˈpʰojʃ]

The aspiration appears in **all three voiceless stop positions** (labial, alveolar, velar) and in multiple environments (initial, medial, final). This is phonetically similar to English or German aspiration but is completely alien to standard EP.

Additional Madeiran features:
- Heavy final devoicing: [ku̥ˈz̥ið], [d̥], [v̥]
- Nasal diphthongs: [kˈpw̃ĩɲu] for *copinho*
- Nasalization: [ˈpɾẽdɐʃ] for *prendas*
- Distinctive lexicon: *pimpinhela* (a local herb)

### 2.4 RHOTIC INVENTORY — Full Spectrum

The dataset reveals the complete Portuguese rhotic inventory in complementary geographic distribution:

| Rhotic | IPA | Where attested | Environment |
|---|---|---|---|
| Alveolar tap | [ɾ] | All regions | Intervocalic, clusters |
| Alveolar trill | [r] | Vizela (rare) | Emphatic |
| Uvular fricative (voiced) | [ʁ] | Lisbon, Algarve, some Porto | Onset /ʁ/, coda |
| Uvular fricative (voiceless) | [χ] | Vizela, Porto, Braga | Coda, preconsonantal |
| Uvular trill | [ʀ] | Gaia | Onset |
| Velar fricative (voiceless) | [x] | Gaia | Coda |
| Retroflex flap | [ɽ] | Vizela, Penafiel, Viana | Free variant of [ɾ] |

**Key observations:**

**Vizela/Braga** uses **voiceless uvular** [χ] in coda: *resolvido* → [χzoɫˈviðu], *terra* → [tɛχ].

**Gaia** alternates between **uvular trill** [ʀ] (*razão* → [ʀɐˈzõw̃]) and **voiceless velar** [x] (*responder* → [xʃˈpõdeɾ], *respostas* → [xˈpɔʃtɐʂ]).

**Lisbon** consistently uses **voiced uvular** [ʁ]: *rir* → [ʁiɾ], *resultado* → [ʁzuɫˈtaɾ], *rock* → [χɔk]/[ʁɔk].

**Algarve** mirrors Lisbon: *resido* → [ʁˈzið], *rir* → [ʁiɾ].

**Braga (Cividade)** shows both: *terra* → [tɛχ] (coda voiceless) but *religioso* → [ʁɨlɨˈʒjɔzɐ].

**No region uses an alveolar trill [r] as the primary realization of /ʁ/**, confirming the near-total uvularization of Portuguese across all surveyed dialects.

### 2.5 VOWEL REDUCTION — North vs. South Gradient

Northern Portuguese is famous for extreme unstressed vowel reduction, and the data confirms a clear **north-to-south gradient**:

#### Extreme reduction (Northern interior — Vizela, Alvarenga, Braga):

Entire syllables are deleted, vowels fully devoiced or elided:
- *Portugal* → [pɾ̥tʰˈɣaɫ] (2 syllables lost)
- *pessoas* → [pˈsowɐʃ] / [pˈsoɐʃ] (first vowel deleted)
- *problema* → [ˈpɾlæmɐ] (first vowel deleted)
- *porque* → [pu̥ɾ̥k] (full devoicing)
- *processo* → [pɾ̥u̥ˈsɛs] (devoicing)
- *simplesmente* → [sĩplʒˈmẽȷt̃] (vowel + consonant deleted)
- *socializado* → [susjɐliˈzad̥] (heavy reduction)

#### Moderate reduction (Porto city, Gaia):

- *portanto* → [pˈtɐ̃t] / [pˈtɐ̃tu] (first vowel lost but pattern is less extreme)
- *perdidos* → [pɾˈdid̥ʃ]
- *professores* → [pɾ̥ufˈsoɾɨʃ]

#### Light reduction (Lisbon):

Unstressed vowels reduce to [ɨ] but are rarely fully deleted:
- *faculdade* → [fɐkuɫˈðaðɨ] (all syllables preserved)
- *experiência* → [ʃˈpɾȷẽ̃sjɐ] (some reduction but syllable structure maintained)

#### Minimal reduction (Azores, Madeira):

Vowels tend to be more fully articulated:
- *Açores* → [ɐˈsoɾɨʃ] (Azores: full vowel in first syllable)
- *maravilhoso* → [mɐɾɐvˈʎoz̥u̥] (Azores: partial)
- *cozinheira* → [kuzɨˈɲa] (Madeira: vowels preserved)

---

### 2.6 NASAL VOWEL/DIPHTHONG VARIATION

#### Standard mainland nasal diphthongs:
- /ɐ̃w̃/ → [ɐ̃w̃] (Lisbon, Porto, Braga): *não* → [nɐ̃w̃]

#### Azorean distinctive nasals:
**Terceira** consistently shows [õw̃] where the mainland has [ɐ̃w̃]:
- *não* → [nõw̃] (3 occurrences: nõw̃ nõw̃ viˈvemuʒ...)
- This raised nasal diphthong [õw̃] is a **diagnostic Azorean feature**.

#### Madeira:
- Distinctive nasal quality in some words but closer to mainland patterns

#### Northern variation:
- *Alvarenga* alternates [nɐ̃] / [nɐ̃w̃] / [nõ]: inconsistent, possibly reflecting rapid speech

---

### 2.7 SIBILANT SYSTEMS

#### Northern apicoalveolar [s̺, z̺] vs. Southern predorsal [s̻, z̻]:

The transcriptions don't systematically distinguish these, but we can infer from other features:
- Northern samples (Vizela, Braga, Porto) show sibilants in environments where retroflex variants also appear
- Southern samples (Lisbon, Algarve) show consistent [s, z] without retroflex alternants

#### Palatal sibilant allophony:

Final /ʃ/ before voiced onset → [ʒ] is universal across all dialects:
- *amigos* → [ɐˈmiɣuʒ] (all regions)

But before voiceless onset → [ʃ] is also universal:
- *pessoas* → [pˈsoɐʃ] (all regions)

---

### 2.8 LATERAL VARIATION

#### Dark [ɫ] / Velarized [l̴]

All regions show velarized laterals in coda position, but with varying degrees:

**Porto/Braga (heaviest velarization):**
- *Portugal* → [pɾ̥tʰˈɣaɫ]
- *social* → [sjaɫ]
- *condicional* → [kõdisjuˈnaɫ]

**Lisbon:**
- *festival* → [fʃtiˈvaɫ]
- *local* → [luˈkaɫ]

**Porto (Miragaia):** Uses [l̴] notation: *faculdade* → [fɐkul̴ˈdaðɨ], *filme* → [ˈfil̴m]

#### Palatalized lateral [ʎ]:

Preserved in ALL regions sampled — the merger of /ʎ/ → /j/ (yeísmo), which is widespread in Brazilian Portuguese, is NOT attested in any European sample here.

---

### 2.9 VOICED STOP LENITION → APPROXIMANTS

The allophonic rule /b, d, ɡ/ → [β, ð, ɣ] in non-initial/post-nasal position is **universal** across all dialects but varies in intensity:

**Northern (strongest lenition — frequent full elision):**
- *todos* → [tod̥ʃ] (devoicing + near elision)
- *video* → [ˈβiðju]
- *agora* → [ɐˈɣɔɾɐ]

**Lisbon (moderate):**
- *eventos* → [iˈvẽtu̥ʃ]
- *pronto* → [ˈpɾɔ̃tu̥]

**Madeira (conservative — less lenition):**
- Stops sometimes preserved as full plosives: *bolo* → [ˈbolu], *galo* → [ˈɡalu]

### 2.10 SPECIFIC REGIONAL FEATURES

#### Azores (Terceira) — Unique Features:
1. **[õw̃] for /ɐ̃w̃/:** *não* → [nõw̃] — raised nasal diphthong
2. **[mũt] for /mũjtʰ/:** *muito* → [mũt] — simplified, no palatal glide
3. **Preserved full vowels** in unstressed syllables: less reduction than mainland
4. **[ˈɔvɛs] for *houvesse*:** distinctive vowel quality
5. **Slower speech rhythm** perceptible from fewer elisions

#### Algarve (Quarteira) — Unique Features:
1. **Southern [ɐ] quality:** closer to open central, not northern raised
2. **Consistent [ʁ]** for /ʁ/ (no uvular trill or velar variants)
3. **Less vowel reduction** than north but more than Lisbon standard
4. **[ɐ̃ȷ̃] nasal diphthong** in *em* contexts: standard southern pattern

#### Portalegre (Crato) — Transitional:
1. **Diphthongization [ɛ͡ɐ]:** *checa* → [ˈʃɛ͡ɐkɐ] — shares Alfena's breaking pattern
2. Intermediate vowel reduction (between north and south)
3. This confirms the **transitional zone** of Alto Alentejo between northern and southern dialects

#### Lisbon (Castelo, S. Pedro) — Standard Reference:
1. **[ɨ] for unstressed /e/:** consistent centralization
2. **[ʁ] uvular fricative** dominant
3. **Moderate vowel reduction** but syllable structure preserved
4. **No retroflex consonants**
5. **[ðˡaðɨ]** in S. Pedro sample: lateral release on stops (minor feature)
6. **[iɨ] hiatus filler** between utterances: *e* → [iɨ] — extremely frequent as discourse marker

## 5. Prosodic Features

### 5.1 Speech Rate and Vowel Elision

The transcriptions implicitly encode **speech rate** via the degree of vowel reduction and syllable deletion. Ranking from most to least rapid/reduced:

1. **Vizela, Braga** — most extreme elision (pɾ̥tʰˈɣaɫ, pˈsowɐʃ)
2. **Alvarenga** — very heavy (ˈtɐmuʐɐ, ˈtemuʐ)
3. **Miragaia/Porto city** — heavy (pˈtɐ̃t, dfˈniðɐʃ)
4. **Gaia** — moderate-heavy (pɾˈdid̥ʃ)
5. **Penafiel** — moderate (pˈkenw, dˈpojʃ)
6. **Lisbon** — moderate (fɐkuɫˈðaðɨ — syllables preserved)
7. **Azores** — light (full vowels preserved)
8. **Madeira** — light-moderate (vowels preserved but some reduction)

### 5.2 Discourse Markers

**Lisbon [iɨ]:** The filler *e* → [iɨ] / [iɨmː] appears repeatedly in the Lisbon samples as a prolonged discourse marker, sometimes with nasal quality. This is a recognized sociolinguistic marker of educated Lisbon speech.

**Northern [pa]:** *pá* → [pa] appears in Porto/Braga samples as the quintessential northern discourse particle (from *rapaz*).

## 7. Implications for Phonemizer/TTS Training

These dialect features have direct implications for grapheme-to-phoneme (G2P) systems:

1. **A single EP G2P model will fail** for northern rural speech (retroflex alternation, extreme reduction)
2. **Madeiran requires its own aspiration module** — no mainland model predicts [tʰ, pʰ, kʰ]
3. **Azorean nasal diphthongs** require a distinct vowel mapping: /ɐ̃w̃/ → [õw̃]
4. **Alfena-type vowel breaking** is highly localized but phonetically dramatic — a model trained on Lisbon data will completely miss [ɛ͡ɐ]
5. The **retroflex ↔ standard sibilant** alternation in northern speech is conditioned by prosodic environment and speech rate, not purely by phonological context — making it difficult to model rule-based
6. **Vowel reduction** ranges from near-zero (Azores) to near-total deletion (Vizela) — any phonemizer must parametrize this on a continuum
