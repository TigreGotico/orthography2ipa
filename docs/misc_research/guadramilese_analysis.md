# Guadramilês: Linguistic Analysis, Dictionary & Grammar

## From a Single Oral Testimony of a Nearly Extinct Asturleonese Dialect

---

## 1. Source Text: Annotated Transcription

The text below is an oral account of traditional wedding customs in Guadramil, Bragança district, northeastern Portugal. It was transcribed using European Portuguese orthographic conventions, as Guadramilês has no standardized writing system. The speaker is an elderly woman recalling practices from her youth.

> **Antigamente**, mataba-se uma **bitela**, cumbidaba-se na **béspera** do casamento, cumbidaba-se a gente toda, três dias antes do casamento. Eram os noivos que iam cumbidar… **Juntában-se** – espera tu, que **tamien** agora sou **you**! – juntaba-se… o **noibo** i a **noiba** i entraba por a casa da família i **diç**: "**Manhana bamos-mos** a casar…, fazemos o gusto que **mos baias acumpanhar**." **Salíen** daquela **diban** outra – cumo os ciganos a pedir **la smola** – i **dezien-la** na mesma. No **pormanhana** da **fiesta** da, da **boda** **tornában** a chamar-los: "**Yá** que já está o **almuorço** feito i **bá-s'a** comer" (…) mientras **stiberan** a **cumbersar**, os outros **díban** a comer. Eles **stában** lá… **ayunar**, hum? I díban para igreja a con, a, a casar-se. **An ayunas!** An ayunas! I ao despois o cura staba no altar. I diba o noibo por, a, a noiba porque o noibo **yá** estava por lado dos homes. I não iam juntos. Não! A noiba diba **cuna** madrina i o noibo diba **cuno** padrino. I juntában-se. Não! Ao contrário! Sim! Escuta! O noivo ia com a madrinha e a noiva com o padrinho. É ao contrário, filha. **Bá**. I ao depois, juntában-se à borda do altar – **enton inda**, enton inda **nun** habia **aneles** – i ao despois punha, agarraba-l' a **mã**, o cura, i punha-la…, ataba-las assi i diç el: "Tu, Joan, tu estimas a Maria por tua esposa?" El dezia-le que si. Mas despois el dezia-le para Maria: "Maria, tu estimas o João por teu esposo?" I ela dezia que si porque tinha-le vontade. Depois a, ataba-l' a estola i rezaba-l' **eili** quatro **mentiras** i, i tiraba-se (…) Pronto, estava casado. (…) Ao **benir** pa casa, diba gente da… que cumbidou pa jantar, pa boda, diba l'acompanhar **atá** casa. **Tenia** um par de quilos de **almendras** i ao depois daba-los a cada, a cada pessona, una ou duas ou três ou quatro. I **dában-le bin**, **bino** i **pan**. I os que **iéran** da boda **fazien** baile i **bailaban**, os que **num** iéran da boda diban para **su** casa. Pronto. **Puis** iera assi.

## 2. Phonological Analysis

### 2.1 Consonant Features

| Feature | Guadramilês | Portuguese | Spanish/Leonese | Examples from text |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **B/V merger** (betacism) | /b/ for both | /b/ vs /v/ | /b/ for both | *noibo, noiba, bamos, bin, bino, boda, benir, baias, béspera* |
| **Initial L- palatalization** | /ʎ/ | /l/ | /ʎ/ (Leonese) | *la smola* (← l'esmola); cf. description: *lhuna* |
| **Final -ç for -z** | /θ/ or /s/ | /ʃ/ | /θ/ | *diç* (= diz "says") |
| **Retention of -n-** | preserved | lost (nasalizes vowel) | preserved | *una, pan, bin* (vs. Pt. *uma, pão, vinho*) |
| **F- retention** | /f/ | /f/ | /f/ (Sp.), /h/ (old Cast.) | *fiesta, fazien* |
| **S- + consonant clusters** | retained | add /ɨ/ | retained | *smola, stában, stiberan* |

### 2.2 Vowel Features

| Feature | Guadramilês | Portuguese | Leonese | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Diphthongization of Lat. Ŏ** | /wo/ or /we/ | /o/ | /we/ | *almuorço* (← Lat. *admordium*), cf. *uolho* |
| **Diphthongization of Lat. Ĕ** | /je/ | /e/ | /je/ | *fiesta* (← Lat. *festa*), *iéran* |
| **Retention of final -e** | sometimes | reduced /ɨ/ | retained | variable in text |
| **-ou → -o** | /o/ | /ow/ | /o/ | *you* (= eu), *outra* retains diphthong |

|---|---|---|---|

| PANEM | *pan* | *pão* | *pan* |

### 3.5 Verb Morphology

#### Imperfect Indicative (the dominant tense in the narrative)

The imperfect follows Leonese patterns, especially the **-aba** ending for 1st conjugation (vs. Portuguese -ava):

| Person | 1st conj. (-ar) | 2nd/3rd conj. | Examples |
|---|---|---|---|
| 3sg | **-aba** | **-ia** | *mataba, cumbidaba, juntaba, entraba, diba, tenia* |
| 3pl | **-ában** | **-íen / -ían** | *juntában, tornában, dában, stában; salíen, fazíen, dezíen* |

**Key observations:**

- The 3pl marker **-n** is a hallmark Leonese feature (Pt. uses -m: *estavam* vs. Guadr. *stában*).
- B/V merger applies systematically: *-aba* not *-ava*.
- The 3pl of 2nd/3rd conjugation shows **-íen** (cf. Leon. *-ían/ién*, Pt. *-iam*): *salíen, fazíen, dezíen, iéran*.

#### Preterite (Simple Past)

| Person | Example | Portuguese equiv. |
|---|---|---|
| 3sg | **cumbidou** | convidou |
| 3pl | **iéran** | eram (or foram) |

**Note:** *iéran* shows Leonese diphthongization of Latin Ĕ in *ĕrant*.

#### Present Indicative

| Person | Example | Portuguese equiv. |
|---|---|---|
| 1sg | **sou** | sou |
| 1pl | **bamos, fazemos** | vamos, fazemos |
| 3sg | **diç** (= diz), **está** | diz, está |

#### Imperative / Subjunctive

| Form | Example | Meaning |
|---|---|---|
| 2sg subjunctive | **baias** | (that you) go — *fazemos o gusto que mos baias acumpanhar* |

#### Irregular Verbs Attested

| Infinitive | Forms attested | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **dir** (to go) | *diba, díban* (impf.); *bamos* (pres.) | Suppletive: *ir/dir* stem for imperfect (Leonese *dir* = Pt. *ir*) |
| **star** (to be) | *staba, stában, stiberan* | Leonese *estar* without prosthetic e- |

| **tener** (to have) | *tenia* | Leonese *tener* (Pt. *ter*) |
| **benir** (to come) | *benir* | Leonese *venir* with betacism |

### 3.6 Clitic Pronouns

| Form | Function | Example | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| **-le** | indirect object (3sg) | *dezia-le, dában-le, rezaba-l'* | Leonese *-le* (Pt. *-lhe*) |
| **-los** | direct object (3pl) | *chamar-los, daba-los* | |
| **-las** | direct object (f.pl) | *ataba-las* | |
| **-se** | reflexive | *mataba-se, juntaba-se* | |
| **mos** | 1pl (us/ourselves) | *bamos-mos, mos baias* | Leonese *mos* (Pt. *nos*) |

### 3.7 Notable Syntax

- **Verb-initial impersonal constructions:** *mataba-se uma bitela* (one killed a heifer) — same as Portuguese.
- **Double negation:** *nun habia* — simple preverbal negation, Leonese *nun* replaces Pt. *não*.
- **Ethical dative / affective le:** *tinha-le vontade* — "she had desire for him" — Leonese usage.
- **Article + possessive NOT used:** *su casa* (not *a sua casa*) — Leonese pattern.
- **Self-correction / code-switching:** The speaker switches between Guadramilês and Portuguese mid-sentence (*"Não! Ao contrário!"*), showing the diglossic reality.

## 7. Cultural Notes from the Text

The text describes the traditional wedding ritual (*boda*) of Guadramil. Key customs include:

- **Communal invitation:** The bride and groom personally visit every household to invite guests, going door to door "like the Romani asking for alms" — the speaker's own humorous comparison.
- **Fasting before marriage:** The couple fasted (*an ayunas*) through the morning meal while guests ate, then went to church hungry.
- **Gendered procession:** The bride walked with the godfather (*padrinho*) and the groom with the godmother (*madrina*) — "the opposite" of what one might expect, as the speaker corrects herself.
- **No rings:** In the old days (*enton inda nun habia aneles*), there were no wedding rings. The priest simply bound the couple's hands with his stole.
- **Marriage formula using "estimar":** The priest asked each partner if they "esteemed" the other as spouse — a formula using *estimar* rather than the standard Portuguese *amar* or *querer*.
- **The "four lies":** The speaker humorously calls the priest's prayers "four lies" (*quatro mentiras*), suggesting a wry folk attitude toward liturgical formulas.
- **Almonds and wine:** After the ceremony, guests received almonds (*almendras*), bread (*pan*), and wine (*bin*).

## 8. Methodological Caveats

This analysis is based on a **single oral text** from one speaker, transcribed by a third party into Portuguese orthography. The following limitations apply:

1. **Orthographic ambiguity:** Without a standardized spelling system, the transcriber's choices may mask phonological distinctions (e.g., is *diç* really /diθ/ or /dis/?).
2. **Code-switching:** The speaker visibly alternates between Guadramilês and Portuguese, making it difficult to determine which forms are "pure" Guadramilês vs. Portuguese intrusions.
3. **Single idiolect:** One speaker's usage cannot represent the full range of the dialect's grammar or vocabulary.
4. **Reconstructed IPA:** All phonetic transcriptions are best estimates based on Leonese comparative phonology and the Portuguese-based spelling. Native speaker recordings would be needed for verification.
5. **Tense narrative:** The text is predominantly in the imperfect tense, limiting what can be said about other verbal paradigms.

Despite these limitations, this text is an invaluable record of a language variety at the very edge of extinction.
